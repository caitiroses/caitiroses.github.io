Computer Science

Emily Hines

Mrs. Hines has taught computer science classes at Central Magnet for the past nine years.

  • AP Computer Science Principles

    AP Computer Science Principles is designed to be equivalent to a first-semester introductory college computing course. In this course, students will develop computational thinking skills vital for success across all disciplines, such as using computational tools to analyze and study data and working with large data sets to analyze, visualize, and draw conclusions from trends. This course is unique in its focus on fostering student creativity.

  • Coding

    Coding I is a course intended to teach students the basics of computer programming. The course places emphasis on practicing standard programming techniques and learning the logic tools and methods typically used by programmers to create simple computer applications.

  • AP Computer Science A

    AP Computer Science A is designed to give students a foundation in problem solving through the design and implementation of computer programs. Much of the course is structured around developing computer applications that solve a given problem. At the end of the course, students should be able to design and implement solutions to problems by writing, running, and debugging computer programs that are understandable, adaptable, and efficient. The implementation of these programs is also the tool used for introducing algorithm development and analysis, the development and use of fundamental data structures, logic, and the ethical and social implications of computer science.
